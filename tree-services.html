Tree Removal

If a tree is dead, leaning, splitting, or too close to structures, removal may be the safest option. We evaluate the tree, the fall zone, and nearby hazards, then remove it using the safest method for your property.

Great for: dead/dying trees, storm-damaged trees, trees threatening homes, driveways, fences, and trees in the way of new projects.

What to expect: We plan the cut, protect surrounding areas, and keep the jobsite clean. Ask about stump grinding and haul-off to leave a finished look.

Tree Trimming

Proper trimming helps your trees stay healthy, improves curb appeal, and reduces risk during storms. We remove problem limbs, raise the canopy, and clear branches away from roofs, powerlines (where permitted), and walkways.

Common requests: limb clearance over driveways, pruning for health, reducing overhang on roofs, and opening up sunlight for yards.

Heads up: Over-trimming can weaken a tree. We focus on clean cuts and smart pruning, not “topping.”

Stump Grinding

Stumps can be an eyesore and a trip hazard — and they can make mowing a pain. Stump grinding removes the stump below grade so the area can be leveled and used again.

Great for: clearing space for sod, driveways, fence lines, landscaping, or preventing regrowth.

What to expect: We grind the stump down and can either leave the grindings for backfill or haul them off depending on what you want.

Cleanup / Haul-Off

After trimming, removals, or storms, limbs and debris can pile up fast. We can chip, load, and haul off brush and wood so your property is safe, clean, and usable again.

Great for: storm damage cleanup, stacked brush piles, post-project cleanup, and clearing downed limbs.

Options: haul-off only, full cleanup after service, or combine with land clearing for bigger jobs.
